My research focuses on verifiable geospatial technologies and their relevance to the spatial governance of intelligent machines. The thesis is that credible location-contingent commitments -- commitments to follow location-contingent policies, credible because compliance is mechanically verifiable rather than merely asserted -- are both useful and technically feasible: useful for reducing harm from misconfigured or misaligned autonomous systems, for governance of AI compute, and for consistent governance in under-governed spaces. The most effective credible commitments will require every input to be verifiable all the way back to the sensor or the issuing institution, and that requirement gives the work below its shape.
